Global air traffic 📌

The number of flights performed globally by the airline industry increased steadily since the early 2000s and reached 38.9 million in 2019. However, due to the coronavirus pandemic, the number of flights dropped to 16.9 million in 2020.

Aviation Accidents 📌

The largest loss of life overall in a collective incident is the 2,996 fatalities in the coordinated terrorist destruction of airplanes and occupied buildings in September 11 attacks by hijacking 4 airplanes in the United States of America.

This visualization shows the top 25 geographical regions having the highest number of fatal civil airliner accidents from 1945 until now.

Passenger traffic and revenues by region for 2020 📌

Air passenger traffic: Overall reduction of air passengers ranging from 60% in 2020

Airports: Estimated loss of approximately over USD 124 billion airport revenues in 2020

Airlines: A 65.9% decline of revenue passenger kilometers in 2020

Tourism: A decline in international tourism receipts of USD 1.3 trillion in 2020

Note** Above data are all compared to 2019


Cargo Flights in 2020 📌

In contrast to the fall in passenger traffic, cargo flights surged with the increased cargo-only operations using passenger aircraft.

This can be seen in the graph since March 2020 and a sudden increase in April 2020 with a year over year growth of 35%.
